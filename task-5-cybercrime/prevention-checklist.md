Prevention Checklist

1 Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students in India

2 UPI PIN Rule: Remember, you NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks for your PIN, it is a scam.

3 Verify QR Codes: Treat QR codes like digital cash. Never scan a code sent by a stranger on WhatsApp or Telegram.

4 Use Official Apps: Only use verified UPI apps (GPay, PhonePe, BHIM) and never download "screen-sharing" apps like AnyDesk if a stranger asks you to.

5 Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA on your Gmail, LinkedIn, and GitHub accounts using an authenticator app.

6 Check URLs: Before entering login details, ensure the website starts with https:// and the domain name is spelled correctly (e.g., google.com not go0gle.com).

7 Public Wi-Fi Caution: Avoid checking your bank balance or making payments while connected to free college or cafe Wi-Fi; use your mobile data instead.

8 Privacy Settings: Set your social media profiles to "Private" or "Friends Only" to prevent identity thieves from gathering your personal data.

Regular Updates: Keep your phone's OS and banking apps updated to ensure you have the latest security patches.

Reporting Channels:

National Cyber Crime Portal: [www.cybercrime.gov.in]

National Helpline: Dial 1930 immediately to report financial fraud.
