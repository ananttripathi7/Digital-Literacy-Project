CASE STUDY

Topic: UPI Payment Fraud (The "QR Code" Scam)

What the crime is:
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves tricking users into authorizing a debit from their own bank account under the guise of receiving money.

Step-by-Step Scenario:

1] The Bait: A student lists an item (like a used textbook) for sale on a platform like OLX. A "buyer" contacts them immediately, showing great interest.

2] The Hook: The buyer claims they cannot meet in person and offers to pay via UPI. They send a QR code to the student via WhatsApp.

3] The Deception: The scammer tells the student, "Scan this QR code to receive the payment."

4] The Action: The student, unaware that QR codes are only for making payments, scans the code and enters their UPI PIN.

5] The Theft: Instead of receiving money, the student’s account is instantly debited. The scammer immediately blocks the student.

6] Target Audience: Typically young adults, students, and small-scale online sellers who are comfortable with technology but may not know the underlying protocols of UPI.

Consequences:
Financial loss, emotional distress, and potential compromise of linked bank account details. In many cases, recovered funds are rare because the victim "authorized" the transaction with their PIN.
