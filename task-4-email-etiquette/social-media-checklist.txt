Social Media Best Practices for College Students
The Do’s (Recommended Practices)

Do Keep Your Profile Professional: Use a clear, high-quality profile picture and an updated bio on professional platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your academic interests and skills.

Do Use Privacy Settings: Regularly review your settings on personal accounts (like Instagram or Facebook) to ensure that private moments are only visible to friends and family.

Do Think Before You Post: Always consider if you would be comfortable with a professor or a future employer seeing your content before hitting the "post" button.

Do Share Knowledge and Success: Use your platforms to highlight your projects, certifications, or interesting industry news. This builds a positive digital footprint.

Do Verify Information: As a responsible digital citizen, check the facts and sources of any news or articles before sharing them with your network.

The Don’ts (Practices to Avoid)

Don’t Post Offensive Content: Avoid sharing or engaging with posts that involve hate speech, harassment, or illegal activities, as these can lead to disciplinary action.

Don’t Over-Share Personal Data: Never post photos of your student ID, hostel room number, or sensitive financial information like QR codes or OTPs.

Don’t Use Unprofessional Language: Avoid using "text-speak" (e.g., "u" instead of "you"), excessive slang, or aggressive tones when interacting in academic or professional groups.

Don’t Engage in Online Conflicts: Avoid "flame wars" or public arguments. If you disagree with someone, maintain a respectful tone or move the conversation to a private setting.

Don’t Tag Others Without Permission: Respect the privacy of your peers and faculty. Always ask before tagging someone in a photo or post, especially in a campus setting.
